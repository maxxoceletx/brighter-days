## 3. Corporate Structure Explained

### 3.1 Why a Professional Corporation?

California law prohibits physicians from practicing medicine through a standard LLC or general corporation. A **Professional Corporation (PC)** — governed by CA Corporations Code Sections 13400–13410 — is the only authorized entity structure for a solo physician practice in California. Only licensed physicians may be shareholders in a medical PC.

### 3.2 Why S-Corp Tax Election?

The Professional Corporation has elected **S-Corporation status** under the federal tax code. This means:

- The corporation does not pay federal income tax at the entity level (pass-through taxation)
- All business income flows through to Valentina's personal federal tax return (Form 1040 via Schedule K-1)
- No double taxation (unlike a C-Corporation, which pays tax at both the entity and shareholder levels)
- California imposes a 1.5% minimum franchise tax on S-Corp net income (CA Form 100S), in addition to personal income tax on the shareholder's share

This structure is standard for solo physician practices in California and is consistent with the advice of a qualified CPA.

### 3.3 Why Does Valentina Hold All Three Officer Positions?

**CA Corporations Code Section 312** requires that every California corporation have at least three officers: a President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer (or CFO). However, Section 312 also explicitly permits **one person to hold all three offices simultaneously** when there is only one shareholder.

Because Valentina Park, MD is the sole shareholder of the corporation, she holds all three officer positions:

| Office | Holder | Authority |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **President** | Valentina Park, MD | Chief executive; execution of contracts, clinical authority |
| **Treasurer** | Valentina Park, MD | Financial oversight, signatory on corporate accounts |
| **Secretary** | Valentina Park, MD | Corporate record-keeping, Statement of Information filings |

This is not unusual — it is a standard and legally required arrangement for a sole-shareholder Professional Corporation in California.

## 6. "Brighter Days" Brand and Deferred Entity Plan

### 6.1 Current Status

**"Brighter Days"** is an informal brand name used in communications and marketing. It is **not** a registered DBA (Doing Business As) and is not a separate legal entity. Currently, all legal and financial activity occurs under "Valentina Park MD, Professional Corporation."

No formal DBA filing has been made with the City of Torrance or CA Secretary of State. This is acceptable for current operations but should be reviewed if Brighter Days branding is used in contracts, billing, or external-facing communications.

### 6.2 Deferred Entity Decision

Per project planning decisions, two options exist for formalizing the Brighter Days entity as the practice grows:

**Option A — Rename existing PC:**
Rename "Valentina Park MD, Professional Corporation" to "Brighter Days" (or a variant) and hire additional staff under the existing entity. This avoids creating a new entity but requires CA Secretary of State amendment filings and NPI update.

**Option B — Create a new S-Corp:**
Create a new "Brighter Days" S-Corp (or PC, if clinical staff are employed) alongside the existing entity. This offers cleaner separation but requires full entity setup: new EIN, new NPI enrollment, new payer credentialing, new banking.

**Current decision:** Neither option is in scope for the current practice launch. This decision requires guidance from a California healthcare attorney and CPA familiar with medical PC regulations before execution. The decision is deferred until the hiring timeline becomes clear.

**Action: Do not execute either option without attorney/CPA guidance.**
