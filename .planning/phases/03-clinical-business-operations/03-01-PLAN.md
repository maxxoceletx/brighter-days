**BOOKING phase:**
- Patient self-schedules via Tebra patient portal (or Psychology Today referral)
- Admin (Maxi) reviews new booking within 24 hours

**PRE-INTAKE phase (1-3 days before first appointment):**
- Run Tebra eligibility check (manual step -- not automatic)
- Document all three Tebra eligibility statuses: Verified (green), Not Determined (yellow), Not Eligible (red)
- Fallback for "Not Determined": call payer to verify manually
- Fallback for "Not Eligible": contact patient about self-pay or alternate insurance before appointment
- Send Tebra intake packet to patient portal containing:
  - Demographics form
  - Emergency contact form (MUST include patient's full physical address for 911 dispatch + county of residence + nearest ED -- per Pitfall 1 from research)
  - Insurance/financial information
  - Telehealth Informed Consent Form (reference Phase 1 deliverable by path)
  - Minor Consent Form Part A + Part B (reference Phase 1 deliverable by path)
  - Financial policy / Good Faith Estimate (reference Phase 1 GFE template by path)
  - Release of Information form (if applicable)
- Confirm patient completed intake forms (Tebra notification)

**FIRST SESSION phase (clinical -- Valentina):**
- Confirm patient location per patient location protocol (reference Phase 1 deliverable)
- Administer PSC-17 and/or PHQ-A (ages 11-17 for depression screening)
- Administer CRAFFT v2.1 (ages 12+ for substance use screening)
- Full psychiatric evaluation
- C-SSRS clinician-administered (suicide risk baseline)
- Document in Tebra: assessment, diagnosis codes, treatment plan
- If controlled substance anticipated: reference SOP-02 CURES check

**Roles and Responsibilities table:** Use the admin/clinical duty split from research (booking, eligibility, forms = Maxi; session, documentation, orders = Valentina; diagnosis + CPT codes = Maxi enters, Valentina approves).

**Decision Points section:** Include decision trees for:
- Eligibility check returns "Not Determined" -- call payer vs. proceed
- Eligibility check returns "Not Eligible" -- self-pay pathway vs. reschedule
- Intake forms incomplete at session time -- proceed vs. reschedule
- Patient is under 18 -- additional minor consent and parent involvement steps
- Patient needs controlled substance -- trigger SOP-02

**Documentation Requirements:** Specify what must be recorded in Tebra and when (within 24h of session).

**References section:** Cross-link to all Phase 1 and Phase 2 upstream deliverables by file path.

**Screening instruments recommendation (include as appendix or subsection):**
- PHQ-A (PHQ-9 Modified for Adolescents): ages 11-17, depression, 9 items, free (NIMH)

- C-SSRS Youth version: ages 5+, suicide risk, clinician-administered, free (Columbia University)

Note in SOP that Tebra does not have psychiatric screening tools pre-built -- PHQ-A and PSC-17 can be added as supplemental forms in Tebra, while C-SSRS is clinician-administered during session.

  <action>
Create the complete CURES Database Check SOP using the standard SOP heading structure.

Owner: Valentina Park, MD (prescriber-only task -- admin cannot perform CURES checks).

**Purpose:** Ensure compliance with CA Health and Safety Code 11165.4 mandatory use law requiring CURES PDMP check before every controlled substance prescription.

**Scope:** Applies every time Valentina prescribes or continues a Schedule II-V controlled substance. Per locked decision: conservative prescribing posture (SSRIs, SNRIs, non-stimulant ADHD meds first), but CURES SOP must cover full Schedule II-V requirements from day one.

**Procedure steps (from research):**
1. Log in to CURES 2.0 at https://cures.doj.ca.gov/ (DEA credentials, MFA)
2. Search patient: first name, last name, DOB
3. Enter date range for Patient Activity Report (PAR)
4. Review PAR: look for multiple prescribers, pharmacy patterns, controlled substance history, high-dose opioids, benzo+opioid combinations
5. Document CURES check in Tebra clinical note (use the documentation template from research: date, time, query date range, findings, clinical decision, provider name + DEA)
6. If red flags found: do not prescribe without clinical justification documented; consider contacting prior prescriber, requesting pharmacy records, discussing with patient

**Timing rules (CRITICAL -- per Pitfall 2):**
- First-time Rx for any Schedule II-IV: check CURES no earlier than 24 hours (or previous business day) before prescribing
- Continuing treatment: check at least every 6 months
- Each subsequent Rx: check within 24 hours / previous business day before each Rx
- CURES check must happen at the appointment where the Rx decision is made, NOT days in advance

**Decision Points:**
- PAR shows no concerning patterns -> proceed with prescription
- PAR shows multiple prescribers for same substance class -> document rationale before prescribing
- PAR shows high-risk combination (benzo + opioid) -> do not prescribe without peer consultation
- CURES system is down (technical difficulties) -> 5-day supply allowed; document the outage; check CURES as soon as system is restored

**Exemptions section (from research):**
- Patient admitted to inpatient hospital or clinic
- Emergency department: non-refillable 7-day supply
- Post-surgical acute pain: non-refillable 5-day supply
- Patient in hospice care
- Technical difficulties accessing CURES: 5-day supply; document outage

**Non-compliance consequences:** Referral to Medical Board of California for administrative sanctions.

**Documentation Requirements:** Include the CURES documentation template from research as an appendix (date, time, query date range, substances reviewed, findings, clinical decision, provider DEA).

**References:** CA H&S Code 11165.4, Medical Board of CA mandatory use guidelines, DEA registration FP3833933.

Format: Numbered steps with checkboxes. Professional tone, no jargon without definition.
