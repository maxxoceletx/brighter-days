# SOP-03: Telehealth Crisis Protocol — Youth Patients

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-03-01
**Owner:** Valentina Park, MD (clinical lead); Maximilian Park (admin support during crisis events)
**Review Cycle:** Annual (or following any crisis event)

---

## Purpose

Define the exact sequence of clinical actions when a youth patient presents in crisis during a telehealth session, ensuring a consistent, legally compliant response without real-time improvisation. This SOP covers pre-session safety setup, three-tier escalation, telehealth-specific edge cases, CANRA mandatory reporting, and post-crisis documentation.

---

## Scope

Applies to all telehealth sessions with patients under age 18. Applies to Dr. Park and any covering provider. Effective from first patient session.

### Section 1: Pre-Session Safety Setup (Collected at Intake)

The following information must be collected at patient intake (cross-reference SOP-01) and stored in Tebra patient record. This data is the foundation of any crisis response — do not start seeing a patient without it.

#### Required Intake Fields for Crisis Readiness

- [ ] Patient's full physical home address (street, city, state, zip — needed for 911 dispatch)
- [ ] Patient's county of residence (for county-specific crisis routing and MRSS dispatch)
- [ ] Primary parent/guardian: full name, phone number, relationship, authorization status for crisis notification
- [ ] Secondary emergency contact: name, phone number, relationship
- [ ] Patient's local emergency number if different from 911 (rare but document if applicable)
- [ ] Nearest hospital / emergency department to patient's home address

**Where to store:** All fields in Tebra patient demographics. Emergency contact fields: Tebra "Emergency Contacts" section. Home address: Tebra patient demographics.

**Responsibility:** Maxi (intake admin) at patient enrollment; Valentina confirms at first session.

### Section 2: Parent Presence by Age Group

At the start of every session, confirm parent/guardian presence or availability per the following table. This is a safety standard, not optional.

| Age Group | Parent Requirement | Action at Session Start |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Under 8 | Parent present in the room or immediately adjacent | Confirm: "Is [parent name] with you right now?" If not, ask parent to join before proceeding |
| 8-12 | Parent in the home and available to enter session | Confirm: "Is [parent name] home and available if we need them?" If not reachable, flag in session note |
| 13-17 | Session starts alone; parent available to join on request | Confirm: "Is [parent name] available by phone or nearby if I need to reach them today?" Document response |

**Source note:** AACAP Telepsychiatry Policy Statement 2017; MEDIUM confidence — no single authoritative standard by exact age bracket. This represents current best-practice guidance.

**Session-start script (13-17):**
> "Before we begin, I want to confirm — is your parent or guardian available by phone today if I need to reach them? I may need to contact them depending on what we discuss."

### Section 3: Crisis Tier Determination

Administer C-SSRS at any session where suicidal ideation is present, suspected, or disclosed. Use the Screener version (6 items, under 3 minutes) for routine sessions; full Lifetime Baseline version for first session or after any crisis event.

**C-SSRS Reference:**
- Full name: Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale
- Population: Validated for children age 5+; 600+ peer-reviewed studies; recommended by SAMHSA and AACAP
- Versions: Screener (routine monitoring), Lifetime Baseline (first session, post-crisis), Recent (follow-ups)
- Source: cssrs.columbia.edu — free download; no specialized training required; clinician-administered, plain language
- The C-SSRS score and tier determination must be documented in every encounter where risk is assessed

### Section 4: Three-Tier Crisis Escalation Protocol

#### TIER 1 — Passive Suicidal Ideation
**Criteria:** Suicidal thoughts present; NO specific plan; NO stated intent to act

##### Tier 1 — Clinical Response

1. [ ] Administer C-SSRS Screener to document current severity; record score
2. [ ] Engage patient in collaborative safety planning conversation (use safety plan template in Section 7 — Appendix)
3. [ ] Identify warning signs specific to this patient
4. [ ] Identify coping strategies patient can use independently
5. [ ] Review emergency contacts patient will call before acting on thoughts
6. [ ] Contact parent/guardian before session ends — mandatory for all Tier 1 events with minors
   - Script: "I want to let you know that [patient name] and I had an important conversation today about some difficult feelings. I'd like to discuss this with you now. [Brief summary appropriate to patient age and context.]"
7. [ ] Schedule follow-up appointment within 48 hours
8. [ ] Confirm patient and parent have 988 and Dr. Park's contact: (424) 248-8090

##### Tier 1 — Documentation (Complete within 2 hours)

- [ ] C-SSRS score and tier determination
- [ ] Safety plan summary (or attach completed template)
- [ ] Parent/guardian notification: name contacted, time contacted, response
- [ ] Follow-up appointment date confirmed
- [ ] Any referrals or resources provided

---

#### TIER 2 — Active Suicidal Ideation with Plan OR Intent (not both)
**Criteria:** Suicidal ideation with a plan OR with stated intent — but not plan AND intent simultaneously; no current access to means or means are not readily accessible

##### Tier 2 — Clinical Response

1. [ ] Administer C-SSRS; record score
2. [ ] Do NOT end the video session — maintain contact throughout
3. [ ] Contact parent/guardian immediately — during the session if possible
   - Script: "I'm going to ask [patient name]'s parent to join our call. [Patient name], I need to include your parent because I'm concerned about your safety."
   - If parent is not immediately reachable: call parent phone number on file; leave specific message requesting immediate callback
4. [ ] Collaboratively recommend 988 call with patient and parent present on call
   - Script: "I'd like us to call 988 together right now. They can talk with you and connect you to local support."
5. [ ] Recommend voluntary crisis evaluation at nearest emergency department (use ED on file from intake)
6. [ ] Complete safety plan (Section 7 — Appendix) with patient and parent
7. [ ] Confirm plan for immediate supervision: patient not to be left alone until crisis evaluation or provider follow-up
8. [ ] Schedule follow-up within 24 hours

##### Tier 2 — Documentation (Complete within 2 hours)

- [ ] C-SSRS score and tier determination
- [ ] Clinical reasoning for Tier 2 determination (specific language from patient)
- [ ] Parent/guardian notification: name, time, method, response
- [ ] Whether 988 call was made; outcome if known
- [ ] Whether patient agreed to voluntary crisis evaluation; ED recommended
- [ ] Safety plan completed and reviewed with patient and parent
- [ ] Follow-up appointment confirmed within 24 hours
- [ ] Any peer consultation conducted; name of consultant, time, recommendation

---

#### TIER 3 — Imminent Risk
**Criteria:** Active suicidal ideation with plan AND intent AND means; OR self-harm in progress; OR patient discloses imminent plan within current session

##### Tier 3 — Clinical Response

1. [ ] Do NOT end the video call — maintain audio/video contact as long as patient is on screen
2. [ ] Instruct patient to call 911 immediately
   - Script: "I need you to call 911 right now. Can you tell me your address so I can help?"
3. [ ] If patient cannot or will not call 911: call 911 yourself using the patient address on file
   - When connected: "This is Dr. Valentina Park, a board-certified psychiatrist. I am conducting a telehealth session with a minor patient who is at imminent risk of self-harm. The patient's address is [address]. Please send emergency services immediately."
4. [ ] Contact parent/guardian immediately — concurrent with or immediately following 911 call
   - Script: "I have called 911 for [patient name]. Please go to [patient location] immediately and meet the emergency responders."
5. [ ] Stay on video/audio with patient until emergency services arrive or connection is lost
6. [ ] If connection is lost: call patient's number; call parent immediately; follow disconnect protocol (Section 5, Edge Case 1)
7. [ ] Document events in real time or immediately following the crisis — do not delay
8. [ ] Peer consultation call within the same day — required for all Tier 3 events

##### Tier 3 — Documentation (Complete within 2 hours; full note within 24 hours)

- [ ] Exact timeline: when suicidal disclosure occurred, when 911 was called, when parent was contacted, when emergency services arrived (if known)
- [ ] Verbatim or near-verbatim patient statements that established Tier 3 criteria
- [ ] C-SSRS score if administered; tier determination and clinical reasoning
- [ ] Actions taken in sequence
- [ ] Outcome: whether emergency services confirmed arrival; patient disposition if known
- [ ] Parent/guardian notification: name, time, method, response
- [ ] Peer consultation: name of consultant, time, recommendations
- [ ] Provider self-assessment note (see Section 6 — Provider Self-Care)

### Section 5: Telehealth-Specific Edge Cases

| Situation | Immediate Action | Escalation if No Resolution |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| **Patient disconnects during active crisis** | Call patient's phone number immediately | If no answer within 2 minutes: call parent/guardian; if no contact within 5 minutes, call 911 using address on file |
| **Patient location unknown or different from file** | Ask patient to state current address before calling 911 | If patient refuses to give address: call 911 with last known address on file; document to 911 that address may differ from actual location |
| **Parent refuses emergency services** | Document refusal verbatim; assess whether refusal itself is a safety risk | Consult CANRA obligation (Section 6) if parental refusal constitutes neglect; call peer consultant same day; document basis for any independent 911 call |
| **Patient discloses abuse (CANRA trigger)** | Do NOT promise confidentiality before or after disclosure; proceed to CANRA section immediately | CANRA report is mandatory regardless of parent/guardian position (Section 6) |
| **Video freezes but audio continues** | Continue session by audio; request patient confirm current location verbally | If audio also lost: treat as disconnect; follow disconnect protocol above |
| **Parent unavailable at session start (13-17)** | Proceed with session; document parent unavailability; attempt parent contact at session end | If crisis occurs during session and parent still unreachable: use secondary emergency contact; then 911 as fallback |

### Section 6: CANRA Mandatory Reporting — Quick Reference

**Note:** This is a quick reference only. Dr. Park is familiar with CANRA obligations. This section provides operational contact numbers and timelines for use during an active crisis.

**Who is a mandatory reporter:** Valentina Park, MD is a mandatory reporter under CA Penal Code 11165.7 as a licensed physician providing services to a minor.

**When to report:** Immediately upon having reasonable suspicion of child abuse or neglect — reasonable suspicion is a lower threshold than certainty. Reasonable suspicion means facts that would lead a reasonable person to suspect abuse or neglect.

**What triggers CANRA:** Physical abuse, sexual abuse, emotional abuse, neglect, exploitation. Suicidal ideation alone does not trigger CANRA — but parent refusal to allow emergency services for an imminent-risk child may constitute neglect.

**Do NOT promise confidentiality** before a patient begins disclosing. If asked about confidentiality before disclosure: "Most of what we talk about is confidential, but there are some situations where I am required by law to share information to keep you safe. I'll always tell you if that happens."

#### CANRA Reporting Steps

1. [ ] Make verbal report immediately upon reasonable suspicion — same business day
   - Report to: Local law enforcement (LAPD non-emergency: 877-275-5273) OR Los Angeles County DCFS: **1-800-540-4000** (24-hour hotline)
   - Provide: patient name, address, nature of suspected abuse, basis for suspicion, your name and contact
2. [ ] Complete written report within 36 hours
   - Form: BCIA 8572 (Suspected Child Abuse Report)
   - Download: https://oag.ca.gov/sites/all/files/agweb/pdfs/childabuse/ss_8572.pdf
   - Submit to: Same agency as verbal report
3. [ ] Document in patient chart: date/time of verbal report, agency contacted, person spoken to, reference number if provided
4. [ ] Do NOT delay report pending parent consent or patient agreement — report is mandatory and independent of patient/family cooperation

**Immunity:** No civil or criminal liability for mandatory reporters who make a good-faith report of suspected child abuse. Failure to report carries criminal penalty under CA Penal Code 11166.

### Section 9: Post-Crisis Protocol

#### 9.1 Documentation Requirements (All Tiers)

| Item | What to Document | Location in Tebra | Timeframe |
|------|-----------------|------------------|-----------|
| C-SSRS score | Numeric score + ideation type present | Progress Note — Clinical Assessment | Within 2 hours of event |
| Tier determination | Tier 1/2/3 + clinical reasoning | Progress Note — Assessment/Plan | Within 2 hours |
| Actions taken | Chronological list of steps taken per protocol | Progress Note — Intervention section | Within 2 hours |
| Parent/guardian notification | Name contacted, time, method, response | Progress Note — Family/Guardian section | Within 2 hours |
| Referrals made | 988, ED, peer consultant — document each | Progress Note — Referrals | Within 2 hours |
| Follow-up plan | Date and time of next contact | Progress Note — Plan | Within 2 hours |
| Full narrative note | Complete clinical encounter note | Progress Note | Within 24 hours |

#### 9.2 Parent/Guardian Notification Policy

- **Tier 1:** Notify before end of session — mandatory for all minors
- **Tier 2:** Notify during session if possible; within 30 minutes if not reachable during session — mandatory
- **Tier 3:** Notify concurrent with or immediately following 911 call — mandatory

**If parent cannot be reached:**
1. [ ] Attempt primary parent/guardian phone number (from intake record)
2. [ ] Attempt secondary emergency contact phone number (from intake record)
3. [ ] Leave a specific voicemail: "This is Dr. Valentina Park calling about [patient name]. There is an urgent matter regarding [patient name]'s safety. Please call me back immediately at (424) 248-8090."
4. [ ] Document all attempts: time of each call, outcome, message left

#### 9.3 Mandatory Follow-Up Schedule

| Tier | Follow-Up Timeframe | Method |
|------|--------------------|----|
| Tier 1 | Within 48 hours | Telehealth appointment or phone check-in |
| Tier 2 | Within 24 hours | Telehealth appointment; phone check-in if appointment not possible |
| Tier 3 | Same day if possible; within 24 hours | Phone contact; coordinate with ED or emergency services for patient status |

**Follow-up documentation:** Each follow-up contact must be documented in Tebra with outcome and revised safety assessment.

#### 9.4 Provider Self-Care

Crisis events with youth patients are clinically and emotionally demanding. The following steps are recommended after any Tier 2 or Tier 3 event:

- [ ] Peer debrief: contact peer consultant within 24 hours for collegial support — distinct from mandatory consultation
- [ ] No scheduled patients for at least 2 hours after Tier 3 event if operationally possible
- [ ] Document in chart: "Provider peer debriefing completed [date] with [name]" (documents self-care protocol compliance without disclosing debrief content)
- [ ] If distress persists: contact CA Physicians Health Program: (800) 657-2472 (confidential support for physicians)
