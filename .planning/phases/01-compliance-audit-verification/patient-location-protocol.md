# Patient Location Verification Protocol
### Standard Operating Procedure — California-Only Telehealth Services
### Valentina Park MD, Professional Corporation

**Version:** 2026-02 | **Next Review:** 2027-02

## 1. Legal Basis

California Business & Professions Code Section 2290.5(a)(1) requires that telehealth services are only delivered when the patient is physically located in California at the time of service.

Dr. Valentina Park holds a California medical license (A-177690) only. Providing medical care to a patient located outside California would constitute practicing medicine in that state without a license — a serious legal violation that could result in:
- Disciplinary action by the Medical Board of California
- Criminal charges in the patient's state
- Voided malpractice insurance coverage for that encounter
- Payer contract violations and recoupment demands

**This protocol is non-negotiable. There are no exceptions.**

---

## 2. Why This Matters Beyond Legal Compliance

Even when a patient initiates contact from out of state and insists the appointment happen, Dr. Park cannot legally or ethically proceed. The risk is not just theoretical — insurance carriers routinely deny claims for out-of-state telehealth encounters, and several state medical boards have pursued discipline against providers who treated out-of-state patients without proper licensure.

For child and adolescent patients, there is added complexity: parents sometimes travel with children and attempt to continue care from another state. This protocol applies equally to pediatric patients — the minor's physical location controls.

## 3. Pre-Session Verification Procedure

### Step 1: At Intake (One-Time, Each New Patient)

When scheduling a new patient, the intake coordinator (or Tebra patient intake form) must collect:
- Patient's home address (must be a California address)
- Patient's typical session location (home, work, etc.)
- Confirmation that the patient is a California resident

If a new patient's home address is outside California, they are ineligible for services and should be declined for scheduling with an explanation.

**Tebra intake action:** Include the following required field in the Tebra intake form:
"I confirm that I am a resident of California and will be physically located in California during all telehealth appointments."

### Step 2: At Each Session Start (Required Every Appointment)

Before beginning the clinical portion of every session, Dr. Park or her support staff will verbally confirm patient location using the following script:

---

**VERBAL CONFIRMATION SCRIPT — Required at Start of Every Session**

*"Before we begin today, I need to confirm your location for our records. Can you tell me what state you are currently in?"*

**If patient confirms California:**
*"Thank you. Can you also tell me the city?"*
[Record city in session note — see documentation section below.]
Proceed with session.

**If patient is NOT in California:**
*"I understand. Unfortunately, I'm only licensed to practice medicine in California, so I'm not able to provide care during appointments when you're located outside the state. This is a legal requirement that protects both of us. Let's reschedule your appointment for when you return to California. Are you able to tell me when you'll be back?"*
Do not proceed with session. Document the encounter. Reschedule.

## 4. Documentation Requirements

After confirming patient location, record the following in Tebra for every session:

**In the session note (Progress Note or Visit Note):**
> "Patient location confirmed: [City], California at time of service."

Example: "Patient location confirmed: Long Beach, California at time of service."

**What NOT to document:** Full street address. City and state is sufficient.

**If session cannot proceed due to out-of-state location:**
> "Telehealth session not conducted. Patient confirmed to be located in [State], outside California. Session could not proceed per CA B&P Code 2290.5(a)(1). Patient advised to reschedule upon return to California. Appointment rescheduled for [date/TBD]."

## 5. Edge Cases and Decision Rules

| Situation | Decision | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Patient confirms California location | Proceed | Document city in note |
| Patient near state border (e.g., near Nevada or Oregon) | Verify verbally | Ask "Are you on the California side?" — if yes, proceed and document |
| Patient traveling within California (e.g., traveling from LA to San Francisco) | Proceed | Document city from which they are joining |
| Patient is in another state temporarily (vacation, family visit) | Do NOT proceed | Reschedule; explain licensing limitation |
| Patient calls from work in CA, usually sessions from home | Proceed | Document city |
| Patient is a minor and is traveling out of state with parent | Do NOT proceed | Same rule applies; patient location controls |
| Patient refuses to state location | Do NOT proceed | Cannot assume CA location; reschedule until confirmed |
| Patient says "I'm basically in California" or gives ambiguous answer | Do NOT proceed | Must be a clear, unambiguous confirmation of California location |
| Patient is in a CA border city whose name could be confused (e.g., "Reno" — Nevada, not CA) | Confirm state explicitly | Ask "And that's in California?" before proceeding |

## 6. Tebra Documentation — Where to Record

1. Open the patient's chart in Tebra.
2. Navigate to the session/visit note for the current appointment.
3. Add a line in the subjective or administrative notes section: "Patient location confirmed: [City], California."
4. If the session did not proceed: Add a note in the Encounter or visit log documenting the reason.

If a check-in or intake form is presented to the patient before the session (via Tebra patient portal), this confirmation can be collected automatically — include the question: "Please confirm you are currently in California. (Yes / No)" If the patient selects "No," flag the appointment for staff review before it begins.

## 7. Staff Training Requirements

All staff who conduct intake, scheduling, or appointment check-in must be trained on this protocol. Training includes:

- The legal basis for CA-only services
- How to use the verbal confirmation script
- What to do when a patient is out of state
- How to document location in Tebra

**Training frequency:** At onboarding + annually with protocol review.
