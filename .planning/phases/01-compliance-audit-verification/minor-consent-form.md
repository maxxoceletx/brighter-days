### A3. California Minor Confidentiality Rights — What We Can and Cannot Share With You

**This section is important. Please read carefully.**

California law recognizes that adolescents have privacy rights in certain areas of their healthcare. As a parent, you have the right to access your child's medical records in most situations. However, there are specific categories where your child has the legal right to seek care without parental consent or notification:

**Minors 12 and older may consent to the following without parental involvement (Welfare & Institutions Code 5585.50; Family Code 6924):**
- Outpatient mental health treatment or counseling when, in the professional opinion of the attending provider, the minor is mature enough to participate intelligently in such treatment

**What this means in practice:** If your child (age 12 or older) chooses to share certain information in confidence, Dr. Park may be bound by law to maintain that confidence. However, Dr. Park will always encourage open communication between you and your child, and will discuss with you what information can and cannot be shared.

**Dr. Park WILL notify you without exception if:**
- Your child expresses thoughts of suicide or self-harm with a plan or intent
- Your child is being abused or neglected (mandatory reporting under Penal Code 11164)
- Your child poses a serious risk of harm to another person
- A medical emergency requires parental notification

**For children under 12:** Parents retain full access to all treatment information.

**For children 12 and older:** Dr. Park will discuss with your child and family what is comfortable to share, with the goal of keeping you meaningfully involved in care while respecting your child's developing privacy rights.

### A4. Medication Consent

If Dr. Park recommends psychiatric medication for my child, I understand:

- Dr. Park will explain the purpose, expected benefits, risks, and alternatives for any medication before prescribing.
- I must provide consent before any new medication is started.
- For controlled substances (such as stimulants for ADHD, or benzodiazepines): I understand these medications require careful monitoring and that Dr. Park will discuss the specific risks, benefits, monitoring requirements, and our office's prescribing policy before initiating.
- Prescription refills will only be provided following appropriate clinical review and monitoring.
- I agree to safeguard all medications in a secure location away from siblings, other household members, and potential misuse.

### A5. Session Logistics and Parental Role

**For minors under age 12:** A parent or legal guardian must be physically present in the home (or immediately accessible) during all telehealth sessions.

**For minors age 12 and older:** A parent should be available by phone during sessions unless your child and Dr. Park mutually agree that a fully private session is appropriate for a given visit.

**Private space:** My child will conduct their sessions from a private space in our home where other family members or household members cannot overhear the conversation. This protects my child's confidentiality and the quality of their care.

**Technology:** I will ensure my child has access to a functioning device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) with a camera and stable internet connection for each appointment.

**Communication between appointments:** If I have concerns about my child's mental health, medication, or behavior between sessions, I may contact Dr. Park's office at (424) 248-8090 or through the Tebra patient portal.

### What is this about?

Hi. Before we start working together, I want to make sure you understand what we'll be doing and that you're okay with it.

I'm Dr. Park, a child and adolescent psychiatrist. That means I'm a doctor who specializes in helping young people with their mental health — things like anxiety, depression, ADHD, mood, and other challenges.

Our appointments happen by video, which means you can join from your home. Each session is private — it's just you and me talking.

---

### What I do and don't share with your parents

**I keep most of what we talk about private.** I won't tell your parents the details of what you share unless I have a really important reason.

**I do have to tell your parents (or get help) if:**
- I'm worried you might hurt yourself
- I'm worried you might hurt someone else
- Someone is hurting or abusing you

I'll always try to talk to you before telling your parents anything, unless there's an emergency.

**I will talk with your parents about:**
- How your treatment is going (in general)
- Whether I think medication might help you
- Safety planning, if needed

---

### What you can expect from our appointments

- Each appointment is usually 20-30 minutes
- You can talk about anything that's on your mind
- I will listen without judging you
- You can ask me anything — including questions about your diagnosis or medication
- If you ever don't want to talk about something, just say so

---

### Your right to privacy

You have a right to privacy in your care. California law protects the privacy of young people in mental health treatment, especially if you are 12 or older.

---

### If you ever need help right away

If you're feeling unsafe or need to talk to someone right away and I'm not available, please reach out:
- **988** — Call or text, any time, day or night (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
- **Text "HELLO" to 741741** (Crisis Text Line)
- **Tell a trusted adult** in your life
- **Call 911** if it's an emergency
