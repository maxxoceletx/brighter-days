## Your Right to a Good Faith Estimate

You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate explaining how much your medical care will cost.

Under the law, healthcare providers need to give patients who don't have insurance — or who are not using insurance — an estimate of the expected charges for medical services. This estimate includes the costs for the services described below.

**If you receive a bill that is $400 or more higher than this Good Faith Estimate, you have the right to dispute the bill.** See the dispute resolution section at the bottom of this form.

Make sure to save a copy of this Good Faith Estimate.

## $400 Dispute Threshold Notice

**If you receive a bill that is $400 or more above this Good Faith Estimate**, you have the right to dispute the bill. To dispute, contact:

**Patient-Provider Dispute Resolution**
Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
Website: **www.cms.gov/nosurprises**
Phone: **1-800-985-3059**

You must start the dispute resolution process within 120 calendar days of the date on the bill. The cost of using a dispute resolution provider to review your dispute is $25. If the dispute resolution provider decides in your favor, you will have to pay the price on this Good Faith Estimate. If the dispute resolution provider decides in the provider's favor, you will have to pay the higher amount.

## Dispute Resolution Process

1. **Contact our office first:** Call (424) 248-8090 or email admin@valentinaparkmd.com within 30 days of receiving a bill that appears to differ significantly from this estimate. We will review and correct any billing errors.

2. **If unresolved:** You may request dispute resolution from the federal Patient-Provider Dispute Resolution program through CMS (see above).

3. **Your consumer protections:** The No Surprises Act (42 U.S.C. 300gg-136) protects you from surprise medical bills. You have the right to receive this Good Faith Estimate at least 3 business days before your first scheduled appointment.
