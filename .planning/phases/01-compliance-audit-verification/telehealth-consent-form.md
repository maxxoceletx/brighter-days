## Section 1: What Is Telehealth?

Telehealth means receiving medical care through technology instead of meeting with your doctor in person. During your appointments with Dr. Park, you will use a secure video connection on your computer, tablet, or smartphone. This allows you to meet with Dr. Park from the comfort of your own home or any private location in California.

The technology used in your sessions includes encrypted, HIPAA-compliant video software. All communications are transmitted securely to protect your privacy.

Telehealth is used for psychiatric evaluation, medication management, follow-up appointments, and other mental health services that can be delivered through video technology.

## Section 2: Your Right to In-Person Care

**You always have the right to request an in-person appointment.** Telehealth is optional and completely voluntary.

If you prefer to be seen in person, please contact our office at (424) 248-8090 or admin@valentinaparkmd.com to discuss options. Your care will not be affected by whether you choose telehealth or in-person appointments.

**You may withdraw your consent to telehealth at any time**, even after signing this form, simply by notifying our office. Withdrawing consent will not affect the quality of care you receive.

## Section 3: Standard of Care

The standard of care you receive through telehealth is identical to the standard of care you would receive during an in-person visit. Dr. Park is held to the same professional and ethical standards regardless of how your appointment is conducted.

Dr. Park will conduct the same thorough psychiatric evaluation, medication review, and treatment planning through telehealth as she would in person. Your wellbeing receives the same attention, time, and clinical judgment.

## Section 4: Risks and Limitations of Telehealth

Telehealth has some limitations compared to in-person care that you should be aware of:

**Technology limitations:**
- Video or internet connections may fail during a session. If this happens, Dr. Park or a member of our staff will call you at the phone number on file within 5 minutes.
- Poor audio or video quality may sometimes limit the quality of the session.
- Technical difficulties may occasionally require rescheduling.

**Clinical limitations:**
- Dr. Park cannot perform a physical examination through telehealth. If a physical exam is clinically necessary, you will be referred for an in-person evaluation.
- Certain types of crisis intervention may be more limited compared to in-person settings.
- Emergency situations may require local emergency services rather than telehealth support.

**Backup plan for technology failure:** If the video connection cannot be restored within 10 minutes, Dr. Park will contact you by phone at (424) 248-8090 to complete the session by phone or to reschedule. Please ensure your phone number on file is correct and your phone is available during your appointment time.

## Section 5: Your Location Matters — California-Only Services

**Dr. Park is licensed to practice medicine in California only.** This means she can only provide care to you when you are physically located in California at the time of your appointment.

At the start of each session, you will be asked to confirm that you are currently in California. This is a legal requirement, not just a formality.

**If you are traveling outside California:** Please contact our office to reschedule your appointment for when you return to California. This applies to all states, including Nevada, Oregon, and Arizona — even if you are just across the state border.

**If you are traveling within California:** Your appointment may proceed as long as you are in a private location with a reliable internet connection.

By signing this form, you agree to inform Dr. Park if you are ever located outside California at the start of a session, and you understand that the session cannot proceed in that circumstance.

## Section 6: Privacy, Confidentiality, and Electronic Security

Everything you share with Dr. Park is protected by the same confidentiality laws that apply to all medical care. The law requires strict privacy, with limited exceptions (such as mandatory reporting for imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, or abuse of a minor or elder).

**Electronic privacy considerations specific to telehealth:**
- All video and audio during your sessions is encrypted and not recorded unless you provide written authorization.
- Communications sent via the Tebra patient portal are encrypted. Email outside the portal is not fully encrypted — please avoid sending sensitive health information via regular email.
- Your medical records are stored in Tebra's HIPAA-compliant electronic health record system.
- We recommend conducting sessions from a private location where others cannot overhear your conversation.
- Use a personal device (not a work or shared computer) when possible.
- If you use a shared living space, consider using earphones to protect your privacy.

**Risks inherent to electronic transmission:** Despite strong security measures, no electronic system is 100% secure. Potential risks include unauthorized interception of communications, technical vulnerabilities, or data breaches. These risks are rare and mitigated by our security protocols, but you should be aware they exist.

Your health information is maintained in compliance with HIPAA and California Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA). Please refer to our Notice of Privacy Practices for complete details.

## Section 7: Insurance Coverage for Telehealth Services

Telehealth psychiatric services are covered by most major insurers in California. Coverage depends on your specific plan.

**Questions about your coverage:** Contact your insurance company directly using the member services number on your insurance card, or contact our billing team at admin@valentinaparkmd.com.

**Copayments and cost-sharing:** Your insurance cost-sharing (copays, deductibles, coinsurance) for telehealth is generally the same as for in-person visits under California law (Insurance Code 10123.852).

**Self-pay patients:** If you do not have insurance or are paying out of pocket, you will receive a Good Faith Estimate of expected costs before your appointment.
